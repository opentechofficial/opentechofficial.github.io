  1. FixedInternet[1] = "LAN Local Vulnerabilities"

Okay so if you haven't yet read the BrokenInternet[1] post, you should probably read that first, to better understand this post. As we have seen, there are many vulnerabilities with our default local LAN protocols. And I will discuss a few solutions:

IPSec

The first tool I want to discuss is IPSec. As we know, our traffic can often be rerouted, or listen upon, as it is authenticated solely by MAC addresses, and has no encryption. IPSec, allows you to configure your firewall to have direct connections with other devices on LAN which encrypt the traffic using public-key cryptography and symmetric key algorithms, which means if one tries to reroute the traffic, the user will have unaffected access, as everything he does, is encrypted.

Routers

Okay here is a checklist that you should do:

  1. change the default admin password for your router, and store it somewhere safe, never disclose this information
  2. Update your router's firmware
  3. disable PIN sharing
  4. Turn on the firewall if it isn't already on
  5. Set an alarm with google alerts, to alert you if vulnerabilities exist for your router, and upon hearing suchÂ vulnerabilities, automatically update
  6. Make a separate guest network so that anyone who doesn't live at your home or office, doesn't use the same network that you do, protecting yourself by never revealing your wifi password.

Computers

Enable your firewall on your device. And always update software and the OS.
